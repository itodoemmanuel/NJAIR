About NJ-AIR

Our Mission & Vision

NJ-AIR is committed to publishing high-quality, peer-reviewed research in the fields of public administration and innovative research that advances knowledge and practice in Nigeria and beyond.

We envision a world where research drives sustainable development and innovation shapes public policy for the greater good.

Our History

Founded in 2010 by the Department of Public Administration at Rev Father Moses Orshio Adasu University, NJ-AIR has grown steadily to become a reputable platform for scholarly communication and research dissemination in Nigeria.

Through decades, NJ-AIR has published hundreds of research articles from authors globally, maintaining a rigorous double-blind peer-review process to ensure quality and integrity.

NJ-AIR is indexed in several academic databases and is recognized as an authoritative voice in public administration research.

Publishing Process

We strive to make the publishing journey transparent, efficient, and rewarding for authors:

  • Manuscript Submission: Authors submit their original research manuscripts online.
  • Initial Editorial Screening: Our editors verify scope and formatting.
  • Peer Review: Manuscripts are reviewed by experts under a double-blind process.
  • Revision: Authors address reviewers’ comments and resubmit.
  • Final Acceptance: Upon satisfactory revision, articles are accepted for publication.
  • Publication & Indexing: Articles are published online with DOI and indexed for global accessibility.

Benefits for Authors & Readers

For Authors:

  • Wide Visibility: Reach thousands of researchers worldwide.
  • Fast Publication: Efficient review and rapid publication timelines.
  • Open Access: Articles freely available to maximize impact.
  • Support & Guidance: Editorial assistance throughout the process.

For Readers:

  • Access to Cutting-edge Research in Public Administration and Innovation.
  • Reliable, Peer-reviewed Content for Academic & Practical Use.
  • User-friendly interface for browsing, searching, and downloading articles.
